Applications

Apply for a Residency

Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. We accept artists for spring (April–June) and autumn (September–November) residencies.

Apply via Submittable ↗

Before You Apply

We encourage all prospective applicants to read the About and Space pages carefully. The Marradi Residency is a small, personal program — one artist at a time — and we are looking for artists whose work will genuinely benefit from sustained time in this particular place.

What We Look For

  • Practice A developed artistic practice with a clear body of work, at any career stage. We welcome emerging artists as well as those mid-career or established.
  • Project A specific project or area of inquiry that would benefit from uninterrupted time and immersion in place.
  • Engagement A genuine interest in Marradi and the Apennine landscape — not just a desire for a pleasant Italian sojourn, but a real curiosity about this particular place.
  • Self-sufficiency The ability to live and work independently. The residency is not a structured program — it requires the artist to be self-directed.
  • Discipline Open to all — painters, sculptors, writers, composers, photographers, filmmakers, poets, and others.

How to Apply

01

Prepare your materials

Work samples, artist statement, project description, CV, and two references.

02

Submit via Submittable

Complete the application form and upload your materials through our Submittable portal.

03

Review

Applications are reviewed within four to six weeks. We may request a brief conversation with finalists.

04

Decision

Successful applicants are notified by email and invited to confirm their dates.

Application Materials

  • Work samples 10–20 images, pages, or audio/video files representing recent work. PDFs preferred where applicable.
  • Artist statement A brief statement (300–500 words) describing your practice and artistic concerns.
  • Project description A description (300–500 words) of the project you intend to develop during the residency, and why Marradi is the right context for it.
  • CV or résumé A current artist CV or professional résumé.
  • References Names and contact details for two references — colleagues, collaborators, or mentors familiar with your work.
  • Preferred dates Your preferred season and any constraints on timing.

Ready?

Applications are submitted through our Submittable portal. You will be able to save your application and return to it before submitting.
