Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

Eligibility & Application

Who can apply?

Writers (fiction, poetry, creative nonfiction) and literary translators with at least one published work — a book, chapbook, or literary magazine publication.

When are the application deadlines?

  • December 1 for May, June, and July sessions (notification by February 1)
  • March 31 for August and September–October sessions (notification by May 15)

Can I apply with a partner or family?

Yes. You can apply individually (paired by our committee), jointly with another writer, or bring family members including a partner and/or children. Note: no children under 12 (the apartment is on the third floor with no elevator).

What does the application require?

Bio (75 words), Statement of Intent (250 words), Project Summary (100 words), Literary CV, and Writing Sample (5 pages, Times New Roman 12pt). Applications are reviewed blindly.

The Residency

How long is the residency?

Sessions range from 4–6 weeks. Two writers share the apartment during each session.

What are the sessions?

  • May — 4 weeks
  • June — 4 weeks
  • July — 4 weeks
  • August — 4 weeks
  • September–October — 6 weeks

What does it cost?

  • €30 application fee (non-refundable)
  • €100 administrative fee per writer/adult (non-refundable if accepted)
  • €250 security deposit (refundable)
  • Accommodation is free (subsidized by the APS)

What's included?

Fully furnished apartment with kitchen, two private bedrooms/quarters, shared bathroom, small loggia, and WiFi. Access to Centro Studi Campaniani archives and the town library.

What's not included?

Food and groceries, travel to and from Marradi, personal expenses, and utilities beyond basic usage.

Living in Marradi

How do I get to Marradi?

By train on the historic Ferrovia Faentina ("Dante's Train"): from Florence (1 hour 5 minutes, €8–10) or Faenza (45 minutes, €4–5). The apartment is a 5-minute walk from the station. Approximately 12 trains daily.

Is there a grocery store?

Yes. Marradi has a supermarket, bakery, butcher, pharmacy, and a weekly farmers market. Everything is within walking distance of the apartment.

What language is spoken?

Italian. Some locals speak basic English, but Italian is helpful for daily life. Knowledge of Italian is not required for the residency.

Is there WiFi?

Yes, the apartment has WiFi.

Residency Requirements

What are residents expected to do?

  • Attend welcome aperitivo
  • Participate in one community event (2–3 options provided)
  • Donate 2 books to the library (one of yours, one favourite)
  • Complete a feedback form at the end of your stay
  • Mention the residency on your resume or bio

Are there any restrictions?

No children under 12 — the apartment is on the third floor with no elevator.

About the Organisation

What is the APS?

The Dino Campana Literary Residency is operated by a registered Italian APS (Associazione di Promozione Sociale) — a nonprofit cultural association that runs the residency May–October and accepts tax-deductible donations.

Are donations tax-deductible?

Yes, for Italian taxpayers (35% deduction up to €30,000/year). International donations are welcomed but may not qualify for tax benefits in your country.

How is the residency funded?

Through grants, donations, volunteer work, and application fees. The APS subsidizes accommodation costs to keep the residency accessible to writers at all career stages.
