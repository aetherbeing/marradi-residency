The Town

Marradi, Tuscany

A small town in the Apennine mountains between Florence and Ravenna — unhurried, beautiful, and largely unknown to tourists.

Marradi town square
Valley view

A Town Out of Time

Marradi sits at around 300 meters elevation in the Apennine hills of the Mugello, where Tuscany meets Emilia-Romagna. It is a working town of around 3,000 people — with a butcher, a baker, a weekly market, several trattorias, a church whose bells mark the hours, and a train line that connects it to Florence in about an hour.

It is not a place that performs itself for visitors. Daily life here has its own pace — older, slower, oriented around seasons, food, and the rhythms of the surrounding landscape.

For artists, this is precisely the point. Marradi offers genuine immersion in Italian life without the self-consciousness of a more celebrated destination.

The Landscape

The Apennines around Marradi are a landscape of chestnut forests, meadows, ridgelines, and river valleys. In spring the hillsides are green and soft; in autumn they turn gold and rust, and the air fills with the smell of chestnuts roasting in the streets.

The town itself sits above the Lamone river, and trails into the mountains begin at the edge of the village. Walking here is not a sport but a habit — most residents walk daily, and the landscape is always close.

The quality of light in the Apennines is different from the coast or the flatlands — cooler, more changeable, with low cloud and mist common in spring and autumn. Many artists find this particularly generative.

Getting to Marradi

  • By train Direct train from Florence Santa Maria Novella — approximately 1 hour. Trains run several times daily.
  • By car Approximately 55km from Florence via the SS302. The road climbs through the Apennines — scenic but winding.
  • From Bologna Approximately 75km, roughly 1.5 hours by car via the A14 and SS302.
  • Nearest airports Florence Peretola (FLR), approximately 80km. Bologna (BLQ), approximately 100km.
  • In town Marradi is compact and walkable. A car is not necessary for daily life during your residency.
