About the Residency

Time, space, and the slow rhythm of Tuscany

The Marradi Residency provides artists with uninterrupted time to focus on their work in one of Tuscany's most quietly beautiful settings.

The Program

Founded on the belief that artists need sustained, uninterrupted time to develop their practice, the Marradi Residency invites one artist at a time to spend four to eight weeks in the Apennine mountains of Tuscany.

Nestled between Florence and Ravenna in the Mugello valley, Marradi is a working Italian town largely off the tourist path — a place where artists can live, walk, shop at the market, and be genuinely present in a place rather than passing through.

There are no formal obligations, no required lectures or exhibitions. The residency is structured around the needs of the individual artist and their work.

Who We Welcome

The residency is open to artists of all disciplines — painters, sculptors, writers, composers, poets, filmmakers, photographers — at any stage of their career.

We are particularly interested in artists whose work is in some way changed or deepened by direct engagement with place: the landscape, the light, the pace of daily life, the particular character of a small Tuscan town.

Artists from all countries are welcome. Knowledge of Italian is not required, though the experience of daily life in Marradi will of course be richer with some language.

What's Included

  • Housing A furnished private apartment in the center of Marradi, fully equipped for daily living.
  • Studio A dedicated studio space within or adjacent to the apartment, set up to accommodate the artist's practice.
  • Duration Residencies run four to eight weeks, with some flexibility depending on the artist's project and availability.
  • Stipend A modest living stipend is provided to cover food and local expenses. Travel to and from Marradi is the artist's responsibility.
  • Support Introductions to the community, orientation to the town, and ongoing availability from the residency director throughout the stay.
  • Seasons We host two residencies per year: spring (April–June) and autumn (September–November).
