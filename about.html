About the Residency

A Literary Residency in its Infancy

Marradi is rediscovering its literary heritage. The birthplace of Dino Campana — who self-published his seminal poetry collection Canti Orfici here in 1914 — is being transformed into a museum and cultural space over the next 2–3 years.

The community is proud of this legacy and eager to welcome writers from around the world, gradually establishing Marradi as a unique destination for literary work and reflection.

We host two writers at a time for 4-week sessions, May through September. Our goal is simple: to give writers the time, space, and a temporary home to focus on their work — whether that means writing, recalibrating between projects, finding inspiration in new surroundings, or simply resting and preparing for what comes next.

Dino Campana (1885–1932)

The poet of Marradi

Dino Campana was born in Marradi in 1885. A poet who spent years wandering through Italy, Switzerland, and Argentina, he returned to Marradi in 1914 to self-publish his masterwork, Canti Orfici (Orphic Songs) at his own expense.

The collection — mixing poetry and prose — explores themes of journey, landscape, and identity, blending vivid imagery of Italian places (Marradi, Florence, La Verna) with hallucinatory visions.

Largely ignored during his lifetime, Canti Orfici is now recognized as a masterpiece of Italian modernist poetry.
