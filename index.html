Dino Campana Literary Residency

Marradi, Tuscany

Surrounded by green mountains and hills covered with pine and chestnut trees, and cut by the Lamone River, Marradi feels remote — far from tourists and the tran-tran of busy cities. Yet it's only an hour by train from Florence or Bologna.

The Residency

A place to write, think, and be inspired

The Dino Campana Literary Residency supports writers — making sure that, like Campana, they will be inspired, tap the genius of creativity, experience nature and beauty, and pursue their craft and passion no matter what.

Marradi is the home of poet Dino Campana, who wrote and self-published Canti Orfici here in 1914. The residency is named in his honour and hosted in the heart of this quiet Apennine village.

Who Can Apply

Writers at all career stages

Fiction writers, poets, creative nonfiction writers, and literary translators with at least one published work (book, chapbook, or literary magazine publication).

For our inaugural year (2026), we are inviting a select group of writers. Open applications begin Fall 2026 for the 2027 season.
